How to submit an exercise or project

Hans Petter Langtangen (hpl at ifi.uio.no)

Exercises or projects are not accepted as email attachments (too inefficient procedure with storing and archiving files). Instead, all files associated with exercises or projects must exist in some Git repository on GitHub. This is the way people work with software and technical documents in 2014!

  • Get an account on GitHub:
    1. Go to github.com and create an account.
    2. Go to your account settings (icon in the upper left corner of the page), choose SSH Keys, and provide your SSH key. Often, this is just a matter of pasting the contents of id_rsa.pub or id_dsa.pub files, located in the .ssh subdirectory of your home directory, into the Key box in the web page. Make sure to just cut and paste the text from, e.g., id_rsa.pub without any extra whitespaces or other text. How to generate these files is described in the link generating SSH keys above the SSH Keys box.
  • Send your name and your GitHub username to hpl@ifi.uio.no.
  • The INF5620 course has a virtual classroom at GitHub. You will automatically get a Git repository in this classroom. You must put all your exercises and projects in this repository. We have naming conventions for directories. If you collaborate on an exercise or project, you may use the repository of one of your collaborators instead.
A simple need-to-know intro to project hosting sites and version control systems has been written: HTML document or PDF for printing).
