Exam 2016

Xing Cai (xingca at ifi.uio.no)


Four-hour written exam on Tuesday Nov 29, 9:00-13:00.

60-70 percent of the exam questions will be chosen from the following five topics:

  • (1) 1D finite elements for approximation.
    • Approximate the function \( f(x)=x(2âˆ’x) \) on \( [0,1] \) by two P1 elements, using the projection or least squares method. Sketch the approximate solution. Explain in detail how the computation is done.
    • Repeat the above task but using instead one P2 element.
    • Use the interpolation or collocation method with the collocation points \( x=0,0.5,1 \), for both the case of two P1 elements and the case of one P2 element.
    • Generalize the projection or least squares method for approximating a general \( f(x) \) function, with \( N \) uniform P1 elements.
  • (2) Solving the ODE problem \( u''=-\omega^2 u \) by a finite difference method. The initial conditions are \( u(0)=I \) and \( u'(0)=V \), where \( \omega \), \( I \) and \( V \) are three nonzero constants.
    • Derive an exact solution \( u_e(t) \) that fulfills the above ODE and the prescribed initial conditions.
    • Describe in detail a finite difference method to solve the above ODE for \( 0 < t\le T \) using \( N \) uniform time steps.
    • Implement the finite difference method as a Python function, where \( T \), \( N \), \( \omega \), \( I \) and \( V \) are provided as input arguments.
    • How would you verify the correctness of your Python implementation?
    • Is there any stability requirement of your finite difference method? Present your reasoning in detail.
  • (3) Solving the 1D wave equation \( u_{tt} = c^2 u_{xx},\;\; x\in [0,L],\ t\in (0,T], \) with boundary and initial conditions \( u(0,t) = U_0(t),\quad u_x(L,t)=0,\quad u(x,0)=I(x),\quad u_t(x,0)=V(x)\thinspace . \)
    • Show the details of a numerical scheme that uses finite differences in both time and space.
    • Discuss the stability requirement of the above numerical scheme.
    • Show the details of another numerical scheme that uses finite differences in time but the P1 finite elements in space.
    • Compare the two numerical schemes.
    • Design one method, using only finite differencing, for solving a nonlinear variant of the 1D wave equation \( u_{tt} = c^2(u) u_{xx} \).
  • (4) Solving a 1D stationary convection diffusion equation by the finite difference method. The PDE \( u_x=\varepsilon u_{xx} \) is to be solved in the domain \( 0 < x < 1 \), where \( \varepsilon>0 \) is a constant and the boundary conditions are \( u(0)=0 \) and \( u(1)=1 \).
    • Show that the above equation has \( u(x)=\frac{1-e^{x/\varepsilon}}{1-e^{1/\varepsilon}} \) as its exact solution.
    • Use centered finite differences to discretize the equation, and set up the resulting linear system. (Assume a uniform mesh that consists of \( N+1 \) points: \( x_0 \), \( x_1 \), \( \ldots \), \( x_N \), where \( x_i=i\cdot h \).)
    • Write a simple Python program that implements the above numerical method and carry out two numerical experiments: (1) \( N=20 \) and \( \varepsilon=0.01 \) and (2) \( N=80 \) and \( \varepsilon=0.01 \).
    • Prove that the analytical solution of the centered finite difference scheme is of form \( u_i=C_1\beta_1^i+C_2\beta_2^i \), where \( \beta_1=1 \) and \( \beta_2=\frac{1+\frac{h}{2\varepsilon}}{1-\frac{h}{2\varepsilon}} \). The values of \( C_1 \) and \( C_2 \) should be determined using the boundary conditions.
    • Derive another numerical scheme where the convection term \( u_x \) is discretized by so-called upwind finite difference. That is, \( u_x \) is approximated at \( x=x_i \) by \( \frac{u_{i+1}-u_{i}}{h} \).
    • Write another simple Python program for the above scheme and repeat the same two numerical experiments.
    • Find the analytical solution of the upwind finite difference scheme.
    • Discuss the advantages/disadvantages of the two finite difference schemes.
  • (5) Solving the 2D Poisson equation \( -\nabla^2 u=2 \) on a uniform mesh of \( N\times M \) elements that adopt bilinear basis functions. The solution domain is the unit square and a homogeneous Neumann boundary condition \( \frac{\partial u}{\partial n}=0 \) applies on the entire boundary.
    • Derive the variational form of the above PDE in detail.
    • How many degrees of freedom are there in total? How would you number the mesh nodes with respect to the rows in the final linear system?
    • Describe in detail how the basis functions \( \tilde\varphi_0(X,Y) \), \( \tilde\varphi_1(X,Y) \), \( \tilde\varphi_2(X,Y) \) and \( \tilde\varphi_3(X,Y) \) are defined in a reference cell \( (X,Y)\in[-1,1]^2 \).
    • For element number \( e \), how can the physical coordinates \( (x,y) \) be mapped from the local coordinates \( (X,Y) \) of the reference cell?
    • Compute the element matrix and vector for element nummber \( e \), with help of the reference cell.
    • Describe in detail how the element matrices and vectors can be assembled into a global linear system \( \mathbf A\mathbf u=\mathbf b \).
