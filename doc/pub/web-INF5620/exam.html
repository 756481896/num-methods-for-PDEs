Exam 2015

Xing Cai (xingcai at ifi.uio.no)


Four-hour written exam on Tuesday Dec 15, 14:30-18:30.

\( 75\%\sim80\% \) of the exam questions will be chosen from the following five topics:

  1. 1D finite elements for approximation.
    1. Approximate the function \( f(x)=x(1âˆ’x) \) on \( [0,1] \) by two P1 elements and one P2 element, using a projection or least squares method. Sketch or plot the solutions. Explain in detail how the computations are made.
    2. Introduce a boundary function such that the approximation \( u(x) \) equals \( f(x) \) at the end points \( x=0,1 \). Sketch or plot the solutions in this case.
    3. Use the interpolation or collocation method with P1 and P2 elements with the collocation points \( x=0,0.5,1 \).
    4. Generalize the projection or least squares method for approximating a general \( f(x) \) function, with \( N \) uniform P1 elements.
  2. Solving the 1D Poisson equation \( -u_{xx}=1 \) with P2 finite elements. The solution domain is \( 0 < x < L \) and the boundary conditions are \( u(0)=D \) and \( u_x(L)=C \), where \( D \) are \( C \) are constants.
    1. Derive the variational formulation of the above equation using the Galerkin method.
    2. Derive, within a reference element \( X\in[-1,1] \), the three basis functions \( \tilde \varphi_0(X) \), \( \tilde \varphi_1(X) \) and \( \tilde \varphi_2(X) \).
    3. Assuming a uniform mesh with \( N \) elements, calculate the element matrix and vector for each element.
    4. Show how to assemble the global matrix and vector.
    5. How are the boundary conditions treated?
    6. If the equation is now of form \( -u_{xx}=f(x) \), which part(s) of the numerical scheme need to be changed and how?
  3. Solving the 1D wave equation \( u_{tt} = c^2 u_{xx},\quad x\in [0,L],\ t\in (0,T], \) with boundary and initial conditions \( u(0,t) = U_0(t),\quad u_x(L,t)=0,\quad u(x,0)=I(x),\quad u_t(x,0)=V(x)\thinspace . \)
    1. Show the details of a numerical scheme that uses finite differences in both time and space.
    2. Discuss the stability requirement of the above numerical scheme.
    3. Show the details of another numerical scheme that uses finite differences in time but the P1 finite elements in space.
    4. Compare the two numerical schemes.
  4. Solving a 1D stationary convection diffusion equation by the finite difference method. The PDE \( u_x=\varepsilon u_{xx} \) is to be solved in the domain \( 0 < x < 1 \), where \( \varepsilon>0 \) is a constant and the boundary conditions are \( u(0)=0 \) and \( u(1)=1 \).
    1. Show that the above equation has \( u(x)=\frac{1-e^{x/\varepsilon}}{1-e^{1/\varepsilon}} \) as its exact solution.
    2. Use centered finite differences to discretize the equation, and set up the resulting linear system. (Assume a uniform mesh that consists of \( N+1 \) points: \( x_0 \), \( x_1 \), \( \ldots \), \( x_N \), where \( u_i=(i-1)h \).)
    3. Write a simple computer program that implements the above numerical method and carry out two numerical experiments: (1) \( N=20 \) and \( \varepsilon=0.01 \) and (2) \( N=80 \) and \( \varepsilon=0.01 \).
    4. Prove that the analytical solution of the centered finite difference scheme is of form \( u_i=C_1\beta_1^i+D\beta_2^i \), where \( \beta_1=1 \) and \( \beta_2=\frac{1+\frac{h}{2\varepsilon}}{1-\frac{h}{2\varepsilon}} \). The values of \( C_1 \) and \( C_2 \) should be determined using the boundary conditions.
    5. Derive another numerical scheme where the convection term \( u_x \) is discretized by so-called upwind finite difference. That is, \( u_x \) is approximated at \( x=x_i \) by \( \frac{u_{i+1}-u_{i}}{h} \).
    6. Write another simple computer program for the above scheme and repeat the same two numerical experiments.
    7. Find the analytical solution of the upwind finite difference scheme.
    8. Discuss the advantages/disadvantages of the two finite difference schemes.
  5. Solving the 2D nonlinear diffusion equation in the unit square \( \varrho u_t = \nabla\cdot (\alpha (u)\nabla u) + f(x,y, t), \) with initial condition \( u(x,y,0)=I(x,y) \) and boundary condition \( \partial u/\partial n=0 \). The coefficient \( \varrho \) is constant and \( \alpha(u) \) is a known function of \( u \).
    1. Use the backward Euler method in the temporal direction and centered finite differences in space. Explain in detail how to set up the nonlinear algebraic equations per time step. (We assume a uniform mesh with \( N_x+1 \) points in the \( x \) direction and \( N_y+1 \) points in the \( y \) direction.)
    2. Explain how the Picard method can be used to solve the system of nonlinear algebraic equations.
    3. Explain how Newton's method can be used.
    4. If the Crank-Nicolson method is used in the time temporal direction, which changes are needed in the entire numerical scheme? (Suppose we still use Newton's method for solving the resulting nonlinear algebraic equations.)
