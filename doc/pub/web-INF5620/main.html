  • Sep 17: There were two serious errors in Exercise 13 (the errors are corrected in the latest published version of the "Finite difference methods for wave motion" module):
    • The suggestion \( \omega = \sqrt{q}\pi x/L \) has only meaning for a constant \( q \). Use \( \omega = 1 \). With sympy it is straightforward to derive \( f \).
    • The choice \( q=\cos(\pi x/L) \) becomes negative for \( x > L/2 \), which is illegal since \( q \) must be a positive quantity. A relevant choice is \( q=1+\cos(\pi x/L) \).
